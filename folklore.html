FOLKLORE IN COMMUNITY OF KUALA KANGSAR

Kuala Kangsar is the Royal City of Perak Darul Ridzuan, Malaysia, located about 48 kilometers south of Ipoh. Topographically, the Kuala Kangsar area is a fertile area. The dense forest is spread out green. In the middle of the forest stretches the Perak River from Hulu Perak to the Straits of Melaka for 400 kilometers. The river has a number of tributaries that flow into various regions. Since ancient times, this area is an attractive tourist area because of its enchanting natural beauty.

According to a story, the name of Bandar Diraja which is now called "Kuala Kangsar" was a gift from Sultan Muzaffar Shah. Follow this story in the folklore of the Origin of the Name Kuala Kangsar!

In ancient times, Perak was ruled by a king named Sultan Muzaffar Shah. He often travels down the Perak River and the tributaries of the Perak River that stretch in the middle of a dense forest to find a new place to live. One day, Sultan Muzaffar Shah with the treasurer and some of his bodyguards walked along the shores of the Straits of Melaka. When he reached the mouth of the Perak River, he and his entourage stopped to rest and wanted to make a village there.

Sultan Muzaffar's and his entourage went down the Perak River to find a small river mouth. The clear and cool water flows towards the Perak River. Sultan Muzaffar Shah intends to rest at the place to unwind after days down the Perak River.

“Bendahara! Kita berhenti di tempat ini untuk beristirahat sejenak,” kata Sultan Muzaffar.

“Baik, Baginda!” jawab Bendahara yang segera memberitahukan kepada seluruh pengawal lainnya.

While enjoying the coolness of nature, Sultan Muzaffar observed the area closely. In front of him the trees grew lushly, the animals roamed for food, and the melodious sounds of birds broke the silence of nature.

“Bendahara! Sepertinya tempat ini sangat menarik. Tanahnya subur dan suasana alamnya sejuk. Bagaimana kalau tempat ini kita jadikan negeri?” tanya Sultan Muzaffar kepada Bendahara.

“Hamba rasa, Baginda benar. Tempat ini sangat bagus dijadikan pemukiman. Kawasannya rata dan di tebing sungai ada sebuah bukit,” jawab Bendahara.

“Tapi… Baginda. Kita beri nama apa tempat ini?” Bendahara balik bertanya.

Hearing the Treasurer's question, Sultan Muzaffar thought for a moment about a suitable name for the new country.

“Kuala yang ke berapakah ini?” tanya Sultan kepada Bendahara.

“Ini anak sungai yang kesembilan puluh sembilan, Baginda,” jawab Bendahara.

“Berarti, kuala ini kurang satu lagi seratus,” kata Sultan.

“Benar, Baginda!” sahut Bendahara.

“Kalau begitu. Tempat ini kita namakan Kuala Kurang Satu,” kata Sultan.

“Tapi, Baginda! Sepertinya nama ini kepanjangan. Agak sulit untuk mengucapkannya,” kata Bendahara mengajukan pendapat.

“Kalau begitu, kita pendekkan saja menjadi Kuala Kurang Sa,” jawab Sultan Muzaffar.

After that, a new state was opened called "Kuala Kurang Sa" which was ruled by Sultan Muzaffar Shah. Since then, the center of government of the Sultanate of Perak was moved to the new state. A magnificent palace was built on a hill by the river. Over time, the state of Kuala Kurang Sa grew rapidly. Because it is difficult to pronounce the name "Kuala Kurang Sa", the residents changed the word Kurang Sa to Kangsar. Until now, the name of the area or state is Kuala Kangsar which is in Perak, Malaysia.

That is the original story of the name Kuala Kangsar from Malaysia. The above stories include exemplary folk tales that contain moral messages that can be used as a guide in daily life, one of which is the nature of abstinence. This trait is reflected in the tireless attitude of Sultan Muzaffar Shah, despite spending days down the Perak River in search of better new settlements. These traits include traits that are praiseworthy and highly preferred in the lives of the Malays, as stated in the following series of poems:

Wahai ananda dengarlah madah,

Adat lelaki pantang menyerah,

Asal kerjamu tidak menyalah,

Hidup matimu di tangan Allah.
