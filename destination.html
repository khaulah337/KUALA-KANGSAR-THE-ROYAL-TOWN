INTERESTING DESTINATION


Kuala Kangsar has many different interesting location that truly show the unique of Malaysian culuture, here is recomended interesting destination recommended by the community in Kuala Kangsar. These place also being their port to relaxed and doing some activity during holiday or free time.



Ubudiah Mosque

Kuala Kangsar's Ubudiah Mosque The Ubudiah Mosque, with its gold and Italian marble domes, stands out against the Kuala Kangsar environment. This magnificent structure is one of Malaysia's most stunning mosques. If you wish to visit the mosque, make sure you are dressed suitably.

Historical Victoria Bridge

Victoria Bridge is one of Malaysia's oldest railway bridges. It was built in 1897 and named after Queen Victoria to commemorate her Diamond Jubilee. The bridge, which was originally used to carry tin ore until it closed in 2002, is now a famous photo location.

Istana Kenangan

The Palace of Dreams, also known as Istana Kenangan, is located in the Malaysian state of Perak. Since 1986, the Perak Royal Museum has been housed in the well-preserved palace. The wooden structure was constructed without the use of any iron nails, and the walls are braided with wickerwood and adorned with decorations. Its sheath has a distinctive form that mimics a sword.

Mould a traditional Labu Sayong

Kuala Kangsar's emblem is Labu Sayong, a native water pitcher fashioned like a gourd. While some believe the water in it has medicinal capabilities, the majority of the townfolk use it to keep drinks chilled. You may either witness it being created traditionally in some seminars or get your hands dirty and make your own.

Meet the Keris Craftsman of Perak

Keris making is said to have sacred qualities and is frequently done as a family tradition. Pak Mazin, the most well-known blacksmith in town, is the fourth generation of his family to carry on the Bukit Chandan blacksmithing tradition. He welcomes visitors to his workshop to learn about keris manufacturing and observe the process of keris forging.

Enjoy The River Safari of Perak

Kuala Kangsar has a lot of natural beauty, especially along the Perak River. The second longest river in Malaysia, which was once utilised to carry ore in the nineteenth century, is now home to hydropower projects. Consider the sounds of rustling leaves in the air, buffaloes beside the stream, monkeys swinging in the trees, and fisherman throwing their nets.

Watch how Tengkolok are folded

Royalty and dignitaries wear the tengkolok. Men wear tengkolok, a Malay headgear, for big rituals and weddings. It requires a competent tengkolok artisan like Raja Ahmad Akasihah to fold and weave the fabric together with so many various design variants (10 in Perak alone!). He may be seen in the handicraft mart, where he demonstrates how to style and wear the tengkolok to visitors.
